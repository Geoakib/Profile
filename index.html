01 — About

A researcher reading
the landscape from above.

For nearly a decade I have studied how cities grow, how water moves, and how land transforms — using the language of pixels, spectra, and coordinates. My doctoral work at LIESMARS, Wuhan University focused on extracting urban impervious surfaces from open-source spatial big data, producing two novel spectral indices (GISAI and NSAI) now used for automatic megacity mapping.

Today I direct geospatial research and technical frameworks at C4RE, building evidence-based hydrological roadmaps for climate and environmental projects. I also founded Maplicon — a consultancy specialising in remote-sensing analysis and Google Earth Engine automation — and serve as Research Coordinator at the Center for People & Environment.

I believe geospatial science is most powerful when it is generous: open data, reproducible workflows, and findings that reach the communities they describe.
